Yuki-onna

Type:
Natural phenomena-Snow yokai

Features:
Beautiful, mysterious, life-draining or freezing

Appearance:
Pale skin, long black or white hair, white kimono, sometimes icy translucent

Distribution:
Snowy mountains and forests, mainly in northern Japan

Ethnicity:
Native Japanese monsters

Names:
Yuki-onna, Snow spirit, Snow queen

Dynasty:
Originated in the Muromachi period, popular in Edo period

Basic Introduction

Yuki-onna (Japanese: ゆきおんな), also known as Yuki-hime, is a subordinate of the mountain god in Japanese mythology.
She is in charge of winter snow and is a monster that can control ice and snow. She has an extremely beautiful appearance, a cold nature, and skin like snow. She often appears in the wind and snow in a white kimono.

The earliest work that has a detailed record of the snow onna is "Sougi-zokumonogatari" written by a renga master named Sogi Hoshi at the end of the Muromachi period (1336-1573).

Young Yuki-onna will not hurt people, but adult Yuki-onna will freeze their favorite human men forever, and then bring them back to the cave where they live and display them for viewing.
Legend has it that the Yuki-onna often lures men who get lost in the snowy mountains to a deserted place, kisses him, freezes him completely while kissing, and then takes his soul to eat; she sometimes deliberately leaves a frightened human male alive, and then threatens him that if he wants to live, he must keep his "promise" and not tell others about her existence and the encounter with her, otherwise the betrayed snow woman will go to claim the man's life to appease the law of monsters.
Yuki-onna do not care about good or evil, and her weird behavior is just out of instinctive self-protection or pure curiosity. Some people say that they will deliberately seduce men and then freeze them to death, but in fact, judging from many legends, this result is basically caused by their ignorance of the human world but full of curiosity, and their great curiosity about the emotions between men and women in the world.

Behavioral Characteristics

Snowy Tragedy:

In many legends, Yuki-onna appears during snowstorms, approaching lost or weary travelers and putting them into an eternal sleep.

Romantic Legends:

Yuki-onna frequently features in romantic tales. She may marry a human man, but if he discovers her true identity, she will either leave him or disappear forever.

Knowledgeable:

Yuki-onna is closely associated with the forces of nature and the seasons. She is believed to wield the powers of ice and snow, symbolizing the beauty and harshness of winter. Her stories often serve as a warning to respect natures power and to avoid greed or recklessness in snowy mountains.

Fragile, beautiful, and sad are the three emotions that constitute the soul of the snow woman, which is exactly the reflection of the delicate character of the Japanese in the natural ice and snow.

The character of the Yuki-onna is very complex, and it can be said that she is both good and evil; the story of the Yuki-onna is also quite disturbing.
Among Japanese monsters, there are not many unforgivable ones, but many forced ones; there are not many truly hateful ones, but many very pitiful ones.

The Yuki-onna can be a good demon who helps lost people out of the snowy mountains, or a mischievous person who sucks people's spirits.
Her diverse attitudes can also refer to the diversity of human emotions.
Her life of marrying a man makes people feel like a real woman, and it can also bring people into the story and "know the sorrow of things".
