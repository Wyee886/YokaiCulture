Kappa

Type:
Natural phenomena-Water yokai

Features:
Naughty, strong swimming, water dish on head

Appearance:
Green skin, child-like, with tortoise shell, water dish

Distribution:
Rivers and lakes all over Japan

Ethnicity:
Native Japanese monsters

Names:
Kappa, Kawataro, Mizutaro, etc.

Dynasty:
Spread in the Heian and Edo periods

Basic Introduction

Kappa is also known as Kawabata, Kawataro; Japanese: Kappa; Hiragana: かっぱ; it means an animal that lives in the river like a child. The category belongs to water monster. It is known to live in rivers, lakes and ponds.
According to folk rumors, Kappa is thin, about 60 cm to 1 meter tall, weighing about 45 kg, with a bad smell and mucus on its body, making it difficult to catch.

Kappa combines the morphological characteristics of humans, turtles, and frogs, with three anuses on the buttocks. There is a round plate on the top of the head, called a "dish". If the water is poured out or the water plate dries up, they will become weak or even die; they have round eyes that glow and sharp eyes. The nose is prominent, with a keen sense of smell; the mouth is like a bird's beak, with four sharp teeth in the upper and lower parts of the mouth; the limbs are slender; the arms have strong regeneration ability, and they will regenerate if they are cut off; each hand has only four fingers, with webbed fingers between the fingers; there is a shell on the back, and the limbs can be retracted to protect themselves in times of danger.

Behavioral Characteristics

Naughty and mischievous:

Kappa usually likes to play pranks, such as stealing melons or scaring passers-by.

Challenging humans:

They like to wrestle or compete in swimming with humans, but they will strictly abide by agreements and etiquette. I have also heard many legends that Kappa likes sumo wrestling, which seems to be a nature that makes people laugh, but people who play sumo with it are either dragged into the water by it, or driven crazy or sick by it, and basically have no good ending.

Knowledgeable:

Kappa is said to know medical knowledge and sometimes helps humans treat diseases in exchange for food (especially cucumbers).

"Shirafuji Genta, Kazusa, the Sixty-plus States of Greater Japan". The legendary sumo wrestler Shirafuji Genta of the Edo period captures a kappa. 1843-1847. By Utagawa Toyokuni (collected by the author)

The kappa in Hokusai's Manga is based on a turtle. The man in the painting raises his hips high to seduce the kappa who likes scrotums (collected by the Hyogo Prefectural Museum of History)

Kappa also has a very cruel side. It likes women and will seduce women to reproduce for it. After the women give birth to it, it will cruelly kill and eat them. According to Shigeru Mizuki's "The Complete Collection of Monsters: Kappa", there is a saying in the Kumamoto area that Kappa likes to possess young women, and women possessed by Kappa will indulge in lust. Kappa also has a shocking and evil taste. It likes to grab people's buttocks, also known as anal balls. It is said that people who have their buttocks taken away in the water will drown.

The image of Kappa in different periods in Japan is also different.

Sometimes Kappa is a spirit in the water, worshipped by the people, and sometimes it is a messenger in the water, a frost transformed by the water god.

On the contrary, sometimes Kappa is a man-eating monster, it secretly drags people walking by the river into the water, and then cruelly eats their internal organs.

It will also possess children playing by the river. The possessed children will stare at the river every day, and finally walk into the river unconsciously and drown.

A scene of a festival held at the Kawayama Jinja Shrine, which is dedicated to kappa, in Inao, Nankoku City, Kochi Prefecture. The kappa who tried to pull a horse into the water was caught and later rescued by the temple's abbot. It swore that it would never let the villagers drown and was enshrined as a water god (photographed by the author)

In addition to such a terrifying monster image, there are also stories about Kappa benefiting mankind.

There are legends all over the country: Kappa touched a woman's buttocks in the toilet and his hand was cut off. In order to get his hand back, it taught humans how to make a magic pill.

There is also a legend that says that a kappa tried to pull a horse into the water, but was dragged onto land instead. In order to be forgiven, it swore that it would never let anyone in the village drown again. There are testimonies handed down that record the kappa's promise, and it is possible that the kappa was worshipped as the god of water.

Kappa gradually becomes cute

In the Edo period, Japanese monster culture reached its peak, and many painters put kappa, which was originally only described in words, on paper to make it more vivid.

Since the mid-18th century, Japan has undergone industrialization changes, and the Japanese people are no longer as afraid of the word "monster" as before.

After continuous modification and improvement, its image of kappa has become more in line with people's preferences, and the image of kappa has become more diversified in different situations.

In the third technological revolution, with the development of the Internet, the image of kappa has gradually come to the screen, and more and more animations have appeared with the image of kappa.

At this time, people no longer regard kappa as a scary monster, but as a mascot.
